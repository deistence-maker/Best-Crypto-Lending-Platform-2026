## Introduction

Choosing the right crypto lending platform is one of the most important decisions you will make when borrowing against Bitcoin.

A good platform can protect your assets and provide stable access to liquidity.

A poor choice can expose your Bitcoin to unnecessary risk.

This guide explains how crypto lending platforms work, how to evaluate them, and what to look for before borrowing.

---

## What Is a Crypto Lending Platform?

A crypto lending platform allows you to:

- Deposit cryptocurrency as collateral  
- Borrow funds (stablecoins or fiat)  
- Repay later to recover your collateral  

These platforms act as intermediaries between borrowers and liquidity.

---

## How Crypto Lending Platforms Work

1. You deposit Bitcoin or another crypto asset  
2. The platform calculates your borrowing limit based on LTV  
3. You receive a loan  
4. You maintain collateral requirements  
5. You repay to unlock your assets  

---

## Types of Crypto Lending Platforms

### Centralized Platforms (CeFi)

These are companies that manage your loan and hold your collateral.

**Characteristics:**

- Easy to use  
- Customer support available  
- Often support fiat withdrawals  

**Risks:**

- Custodial risk  
- Limited transparency  

---

### Decentralized Platforms (DeFi)

These operate through smart contracts without intermediaries.

**Characteristics:**

- Non-custodial  
- Transparent on-chain  

**Risks:**

- Smart contract vulnerabilities  
- Requires technical knowledge  



---

## Key Factors to Evaluate

### 1. Security Model

Understand how your Bitcoin is stored.

- Is it held by the platform?  
- Is it protected from internal misuse?  

---

### 2. Rehypothecation Policy

Some platforms reuse your collateral for their own operations.

This introduces hidden risk.

Safer platforms do not reuse collateral.

---

### 3. Loan-to-Value (LTV) Limits

High LTV may look attractive, but it increases liquidation risk.

For safe borrowing guidelines:  
https://github.com/deistence-maker/Safe-LTV-For-Bitcoin-Loans-2026.git  

---

### 4. Liquidation Rules

Understand:

- At what LTV liquidation happens  
- Whether margin calls are provided  

For a full breakdown:  
https://github.com/deistence-maker/How-Bitcoin-Loan-Liquidation-Works-2026.git  

---

### 5. Transparency

A reliable platform clearly explains:

- Interest rates  
- Fees  
- Loan terms  

Avoid platforms with unclear structures.

---

### 6. Interest Rates and Fees

Compare:

- Annual interest rates  
- Origination fees  
- Hidden charges  

Lower rates are not always safer if risk is higher.

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Extremely high LTV offers  
- Lack of transparency  
- No clear custody model  
- Reuse of collateral without disclosure  
- Unrealistic promises  

---

## What Makes a Platform Safer

Safer platforms typically follow these principles:

- Conservative lending structures  
- Clear and transparent policies  
- Strong collateral protection  
- No hidden exposure

## How to Choose the Right Platform

Before selecting a platform, ask:

- Is my Bitcoin fully protected?  
- What happens if the market drops?  
- Are the risks clearly explained?  
- Is the platform transparent about its operations?  

---

## Beginner Strategy

If you are new:

- Choose platforms with simple and clear structures  
- Use low LTV (20–30%)  
- Avoid complex or high-risk products  

---

## Advanced Considerations

More experienced users may evaluate:

- Platform liquidity  
- Smart contract audits (for DeFi)  
- Counterparty risk  
- Market conditions  

---

## Strategic Insight

The best crypto lending platform is not the one offering the largest loan.

It is the one that gives you the highest probability of keeping your Bitcoin.

Crypto lending platforms provide a powerful way to access liquidity without selling your assets.

However, platform choice directly impacts your risk.

- Security
